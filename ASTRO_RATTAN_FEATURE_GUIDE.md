## 🕉️ Introduction to Muhurat

### What is Muhurat?

**Muhurat** (मुहूर्त) literally means "moment" or "auspicious time" in Sanskrit. In Vedic astrology, muhurat is the science of selecting the most favorable time for important activities based on planetary positions, lunar phases, and other astrological factors.

**Key Principles**:
- **Timing is Everything**: The right time multiplies success; wrong time can lead to obstacles
- **Activity-Specific**: Different activities require different astrological configurations
- **Vedic Science**: Based on classical texts like Muhurat Mala, Phaladeepika, and Jataka Parijata
- **Personalized**: Can be customized based on birth chart (Rashi, Nakshatra)

### Why Use Muhurat?

AstroRattan provides muhurat guidance for:
- **Marriage**: Finding auspicious wedding dates and times
- **Travel**: Selecting favorable travel dates and directions
- **Business**: Choosing best days for starting ventures
- **Home**: Griha Pravesh (house warming) timing
- **Vehicle**: Vehicle purchase and first drive timing
- **Medical**: Surgery and treatment timing
- **Education**: Starting new courses or exams
- **Personal**: Important life decisions

## ☀️ Sankranti Tab

### Overview
**Sankranti** (संक्रांति) marks the sun's entry into a new zodiac sign. These are critical astrological events that require specific timing considerations for muhurat.

### The 12 Sankrantis (Solar Months)

#### **1. Makara Sankranti** (मकर संक्रांति)
- **Date**: ~January 14
- **Sun enters**: Capricorn (Makara)
- **Significance**: Winter solstice harvest, beginning of Uttarayan (northern journey of sun)
- **Duration**: ~30 days until Mesh Sankranti
- **Muhurat Status**: Generally auspicious for new beginnings
- **Restrictions**: Avoid major events 16 hours before to 16 hours after exact moment

#### **2. Mesh Sankranti** (मेष संक्रांति)
- **Date**: ~April 14
- **Sun enters**: Aries (Mesh)
- **Significance**: Hindu New Year, spring equinox
- **Duration**: ~31 days
- **Muhurat Status**: Highly auspicious for marriages and ceremonies
- **Restrictions**: 16-hour sacred window around exact time

#### **3. Karka Sankranti** (कर्क संक्रांति)
- **Date**: ~July 17
- **Sun enters**: Cancer (Karka)
- **Significance**: Summer solstice, monsoon peak
- **Duration**: ~31 days
- **Muhurat Status**: Moderate auspiciousness; check other factors
- **Restrictions**: 16-hour restriction period

#### **4. Tula Sankranti** (तुला संक्रांति)
- **Date**: ~October 17
- **Sun enters**: Libra (Tula)
- **Significance**: Autumn equinox, harvest season
- **Duration**: ~31 days
- **Muhurat Status**: Auspicious, especially for travel
- **Restrictions**: 16-hour sacred window

#### **Other 8 Sankrantis**
- **Vrishabha Sankranti** (वृषभ): May 15 (Taurus)
- **Mithun Sankranti** (मिथुन): June 15 (Gemini)
- **Simha Sankranti** (सिंह): August 17 (Leo)
- **Kanya Sankranti** (कन्या): September 17 (Virgo)
- **Vrischik Sankranti** (वृश्चिक): November 16 (Scorpio)
- **Dhanu Sankranti** (धनु): December 16 (Sagittarius)

### Sankranti-Specific Muhurat Rules

#### **16-Hour Restriction Windows**
- **8 hours before** exact Sankranti moment: Avoid major events
- **8 hours after** exact Sankranti moment: Avoid major events
- **Reason**: Sun's transition creates energetic instability
- **Exception**: Small ceremonies & personal rituals may proceed with caution

#### **Auspicious Activities During Sankranti**
- Donating to charity
- Bathing in sacred rivers
- Starting new projects (outside restriction window)
- Spiritual practices
- Marriage ceremonies (plan outside restriction window)

#### **Activities to Avoid During Sankranti**
- Surgery or medical procedures
- Home foundation laying
- Vehicle purchase delivery
- Important business meetings
- Travel initiation

### Technical Details

Each Sankranti shows:
- **Exact Time**: Precise moment of sun's ingress
- **Duration**: Until next Sankranti (~30 days)
- **16-Hour Window**: Safe & unsafe periods
- **Astronomical Data**: Julian day, sun's longitude
- **Vedic Status**: Auspiciousness level

### Use Cases
- Avoiding muhurat conflicts with Sankranti windows
- Planning marriages avoiding solar transitions
- Understanding sun's movement through zodiac
- Timing house foundation ceremonies
- Scheduling travel and business ventures

### Astronomical Epoch Data

**Vedic Measurements**:

**Kaliyuga Year** (कलियुग वर्ष)
- Current: 5127
- Definition: Years since Kaliyuga epoch (3102 BCE)
- Use: Long-term cosmic calendar

**Kali Ahargana** (कलि अहर्गण)
- Definition: Total days elapsed since Kaliyuga start
- Example: 1,872,234 days
- Use: Scientific day counting

**Julian Day (JD)** (जूलियन दिन)
- International astronomical reference
- Continuous count of days since 4713 BCE
- Current: JD 2,460,457.5
- Use: Precise astronomical calculations

**Modified Julian Day (MJD)** (एमजेडी)
- Simplified JD used in modern astronomy
- Current: MJD 60,457
- Use: Contemporary astronomical work

**Rata Die** (राटा डाई)
- Gregorian calendar epoch measure
- Days since year 1 in proleptic Gregorian calendar
- Current: RD 754,383
- Use: Calendar conversion mathematics

**Ayanamsha (Lahiri)** (अयनांश)
- Precession adjustment for accurate planetary positions
- Current: ~24.07°
- Meaning: Apparent backward motion of spring equinox
- Use: Converting tropical to sidereal zodiac
