# Phaladeepika — Summary

**Author:** Mantreswara (circa 16th century CE, Tinnevelly / Southern India)
**Translator:** Panditabhushana V. Subrahmanya Sastri, B.A. — Mysore, 1937
**Scope:** 28 Adhyayas (chapters) covering the complete Parashara-tradition of Vedic natal astrology — from planetary natures through yogas, dashas, ashtakvarga, and transits.
**Source file:** `docs/Phaladeepika.pdf` (438 pages)

Phaladeepika ("Light on the Fruits [of karma]") is one of the five foundational classical texts of Indian predictive astrology, alongside Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra, Brihat Jataka (Varahamihira), Saravali (Kalyana Varma), and Jataka Parijata. Its distinguishing strength is the consolidated treatment of **yogas** and **dasha–bhukti phalitam** — practical prediction.

## Adhyaya 1 — Definitions (Sanjnadhyaya)

**Kalapurusha (Time-Personified):** Each sign = a body-part. Mesha=head, Vrishabha=face, Mithuna=chest, Kataka=heart, Simha=belly, Kanya=waist, Tula=groin, Vrischika=generative organs, Dhanu=thighs, Makara=knees, Kumbha=shanks, Meena=feet.

**House nomenclature:**
- **Kendra** (angles) = 1, 4, 7, 10
- **Panaphara** (succedent) = 2, 5, 8, 11
- **Apoklima** (cadent) = 3, 6, 9, 12
- **Trikona** (trine) = 1, 5, 9 — all auspicious
- **Dusthana** (evil) = 6, 8, 12 — the natural malefic houses
- **Upachaya** (growth) = 3, 6, 10, 11

**Sign classifications:** Movable/Fixed/Dual (Chara/Sthira/Dwiswabhava); Male/Female; Fiery/Earthy/Airy/Watery; Day/Night-strong; Uchaya (head-first rising) vs Adhaya (tail-first).

**Exaltation degrees** (Ucchas):
Sun 10° Aries · Moon 3° Taurus · Mars 28° Capricorn · Mercury 15° Virgo · Jupiter 5° Cancer · Venus 27° Pisces · Saturn 20° Libra. Debilitation (Neecha) = 7th house from exaltation.

## Adhyaya 2 — Planetary Natures

Each planet is described as a cosmic significator with a physical body, temperament, and domain:

| Planet | Karakatva (main significations) |
|---|---|
| **Sun** | Soul, father, authority, copper/gold, courage, kingdom, forests, Shiva-worship |
| **Moon** | Mind, mother, mental tranquility, water, fruits, white objects, agriculture |
| **Mars** | Strength, anger, brother, land, fire, weapons, quarrels, captivity |
| **Mercury** | Speech, learning, literature, arts, mechanical skill, friends, sister |
| **Jupiter** | Knowledge, sons, wealth, religion, preceptor, Vedas, ministership |
| **Venus** | Wife, vehicles, ornaments, music, luxury, romance |
| **Saturn** | Longevity, sorrow, servants, iron, agriculture (base), degradation, captivity |
| **Rahu** | Foreign lands, outcastes, mystery, deception (like Saturn) |
| **Ketu** | Moksha, ascetic tendencies, wounds (like Mars) |

## Adhyaya 3 — Vargas (Divisional Charts)

- **Saptavarga:** Rasi (D1), Hora (D2), Drekkana (D3), Saptamsa (D7), Navamsa (D9), Dwadasamsa (D12), Trimsamsa (D30)
- **Shadvarga:** omits Saptamsa
- **Varga strengths:** Kimshuka, Vyanjana, Chapa, Kundendu, Iravata, Paridha, Devaloka, Ghora, Brahma, Airavata…
- A planet in its own / exalted / friendly / mulatrikona / deep exaltation sign gains progressive strength across vargas — 2 own vargas = **Parivartamsa**, 3 = **Uttamamsa**, 5 = **Gopuramsa**, 7 = **Kundendu-amsa**.

---

## Adhyaya 4 — Shadbala (Six-fold Strength)

Six categories of planetary strength, each quantified in Rupas:

1. **Sthana-bala** (positional) — exaltation, saptavarga, ojha-yugma, kendra, drekkana
2. **Dig-bala** (directional) — each planet strong in a specific house: Sun/Mars in 10th, Jupiter/Mercury in 1st, Moon/Venus in 4th, Saturn in 7th
3. **Kala-bala** (temporal) — day/night, paksha, tribhaga, year/month/day/hora, ayana
4. **Cheshta-bala** (motional) — retrograde and combustion effects
5. **Naisargika-bala** (natural) — fixed order: Sun (best) > Moon > Venus > Jupiter > Mercury > Mars > Saturn
6. **Drik-bala** (aspectual) — net aspects on a planet

**Bhava-bala** is separately computed for each of the 12 houses.

---

## Adhyaya 5 — Livelihood (Vritti)

**Key rule:** The profession is determined by the lord of the **10th house**, its Navamsa lord, and whichever of Sun/Moon/Lagna is strongest at birth. Vocations indicated by the lord of the 10th's navamsa placement:

- Sun-navamsa → medicine, incantations, metal work
- Moon-navamsa → water-trade, pearls, pilgrimage, cloth
- Mars-navamsa → fighting, cooking, metals, gold
- Mercury-navamsa → poetry, sacred scriptures, scholarship
- Jupiter-navamsa → Brahmin-service, sastras, puranas, purohita
- Venus-navamsa → music, dance, women, ornaments, silk
- Saturn-navamsa → low-labor, base materials, servile occupations

---

## Adhyaya 6 — Yogas (Combinations)

**Pancha Mahapurusha Yogas** (five great-man yogas) — formed when Mars/Mercury/Jupiter/Venus/Saturn occupy own sign or exaltation in a Kendra from Lagna or Moon:

| Yoga | Planet | Result |
|---|---|---|
| **Ruchaka** | Mars | Commanding wealth, strong body, brave, famous |
| **Bhadra** | Mercury | Long-lived, broad face, intelligent, orator |
| **Hamsa** | Jupiter | Religious, handsome, righteous, loved |
| **Malavya** | Venus | Lord of villages, romantic, prosperous, strong limbs |
| **Sasa** | Saturn | Head of a village, dubious character, wanderer |

**Moon-based yogas** (from Moon's position):
- **Sunapha** — planets (except Sun) in 2nd from Moon → self-acquired wealth, fame
- **Anapha** — planets in 12th from Moon → good appearance, comforts
- **Durudhara** — planets in 2nd and 12th from Moon → many luxuries
- **Kemadruma** — no planets in 2nd, 12th, or Kendra from Moon → poverty, struggle
- **Adhi-yoga** — benefics in 6th, 7th, 8th from Moon → prosperity, fortune
- **Gaja-Kesari** — Jupiter in Kendra from Moon → intelligence, fame, virtuous

**Kendra-based pairs:**
- **Subhakartari** / **Papakartari** — benefics/malefics on both sides of a house or planet

**Wealth / fortune yogas:** Lakshmi, Saraswati, Parvata, Kahala, Vasumati, Pushkala, Goar, Srikantha, Srinatha, Vamsi, Amala.

**Dhana Yogas** (wealth combinations — Adh. 6, slokas 32–36):
Exchange (Parivartana) between lords creates yogas when both lords are "good" bhavas (excluding 3/6/8/12). Parashara-style Dhanayoga table is given for all 12 house-lord pairings.

---

## Adhyaya 7 — Raja Yogas

- **Neechabhanga Raja Yoga** — debilitation cancelled → paradoxical elevation to royal status
- **Sun-caused**, **Moon-caused**, **Venus–Lagna**, **Mars-caused** Raja-yogas
- Strong Moon + strong Lagna-lord → "emperor of the world"
- Yogas producing a famous son / powerful king

---

## Adhyaya 8 — Bhava Phala (Planets in Houses)

Exhaustive 12×9 table: every planet in every house produces its own set of results. E.g.:

- **Sun in Lagna** — bilious, short-tempered, reddish eyes, valiant
- **Jupiter in 7th** — virtuous wife, domestic harmony
- **Saturn in 10th** — delayed but durable success, authority through labor
- **Mars in 7th** — Mangal Dosha; conflict in marriage

Rahu/Ketu behave "like Saturn/Mars respectively, modified by the sign-lord's nature."

---

## Adhyayas 9–10 — Lagna-specific Rules, 7th House, Nakshatras

- Each Lagna sign (Mesha → Meena) gives a distinct body-type, temperament, and fortune profile
- 7th house = spouse, trade, travel; detailed effects of each planet in 7th
- Nakshatra-based fine-grained predictions — applicable via Moon's nakshatra

---

## Adhyayas 11–14 — Life-Events

**Women's horoscopes (11):** Evaluated via 7th house, Venus, Jupiter; different yogas for widowhood, multiple marriages, chastity.

**Children (12):** 5th house + its lord + Jupiter. Yogas for:
- Total denial of issue
- Adopted children only
- Female-only issue
- Loss of children
Remedies recommended to ward off childlessness.

**Longevity (13):** Three divisions —
- **Balarishta** — child-death potential (below 12 years)
- **Alpayu** — short life (up to 32 years)
- **Madhyayu** — middle life (32–64 years)
- **Dirghayu** — long life (64–108 years)

**Diseases (14):** Each planet causes specific illnesses; 6th house = disease-house, 6th-lord placement reveals type.

---

## Adhyayas 15–18 — Bhava Effects, Death, Conjunctions

- Conditions for destruction vs flourishing of each bhava
- Karakatva test: treat the karaka of each house *as if* it were the Lagna and re-apply the same rules
- **Two-planet conjunctions (Adh. 18)** — exhaustive listing of effects for each of the 45 pair-wise combinations (Sun-Moon, Sun-Mars, … Saturn-Venus, etc.)

---

## Adhyaya 19–21 — Dashadhyaya (Vimshottari System)

**9 Mahadashas totaling 120 years:**

| Planet | Years |
|---|---|
| Sun | 6 |
| Moon | 10 |
| Mars | 7 |
| Rahu | 18 |
| Jupiter | 16 |
| Saturn | 19 |
| Mercury | 17 |
| Ketu | 7 |
| Venus | 20 |

**Starting dasha** determined by Moon's nakshatra at birth.

**Rules for interpreting a dasha:**
- A planet gives the effects of the bhavas it owns + aspects + occupies
- Exaltation / Vargottama / Swasthana planet = good dasha irrespective of ownership
- Depression / Combustion / Papakartari = bad results
- 6th / 8th / 12th lord's dasha → difficulties, unless the planet itself is strong and well-placed
- **Antardasha (bhukti)** sub-divides each mahadasha; effects are a *combination* of the mahadasha and bhukti lords' significations
- **Rajayoga kartaras** during matching dashas produce the yoga's full fruition

---

## Adhyaya 22 — Ayur-daya (Life-span)

Three schools of lifespan calculation:

1. **Pindayu** (Satyacharya) — based on Sun's position
2. **Nisargayu** (Jivasarman) — based on Moon's position
3. **Amsayu** (Parashara) — Navamsa-based

**Reductions (Harana):**
- Raja-harana, Bhupa-harana, Ayana-harana, Astangata-harana, Dushtha-harana, Chakrapata-harana
Maximum allowed life = 108 years (human). Kalachakra Dasha applied when Ayurdaya system proves inconclusive.

---

## Adhyayas 23–24 — Ashtakavarga

The **8-fold benefic-dot (bindu) system** — each planet gets bindus contributed by the Lagna + the other 7 planets (Sun through Saturn), graded against each of the 12 Rasis.

**Concepts:**
- **Bhinnashtakavarga** — each planet's own bindu table (8 separate tables)
- **Sarvashtakavarga** — sum of all = 337 max per chart; used to judge overall strength of each house
- **Trikona sodhana** (trine reduction) and **Ekadhipatya sodhana** (ownership reduction) refine scores
- **Ayurdaya via Ashtakavarga** — a specialized lifespan method

**Predictive rules:**
- Benefic planets transit strong bindu-rasis → good results
- Malefics transit weak bindu-rasis → misfortune
- Bindu contribution from each planet individually = highly specific predictive tool for timing

---

## Adhyaya 25 — Upagrahas (Sub-planets)

Nine sub-planets derived from the Sun's daily arc:
- **Gulika / Mandi** (most important; son of Saturn)
- **Yamakantaka, Ardhaprahara**
- Dhuma, Vyatipata, Parivesa, Indrachapa, Upaketu

**Gulika/Mandi placement:**
- In Lagna → evil-intentioned
- Conjunct benefics → less harmful
- In malefic houses → exacerbates dusthana effects

---

## Adhyaya 26 — Gochara (Transits)

Transit results are judged relative to the natal **Moon** (Janma-Rasi). Each of the 7 planets has a specific list of "good-transit" and "bad-transit" houses from the Moon. General principle:

| Planet | Good transit houses (from Moon) |
|---|---|
| Sun | 3, 6, 10, 11 |
| Moon | 1, 3, 6, 7, 10, 11 |
| Mars | 3, 6, 11 |
| Mercury | 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 11 |
| Jupiter | 2, 5, 7, 9, 11 |
| Venus | 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 8, 9, 11, 12 |
| Saturn | 3, 6, 11 |
| Rahu/Ketu | 3, 6, 10, 11 |

**Vedhas (obstruction):** Another planet in a specific transit-house cancels the good/bad effect.
**Lattas (kicks):** Certain stars kicked by certain planets produce special effects.
**Effects in Ashtakavarga:** Transits through one's high-bindu rasis give strong benefic fruits; low-bindu rasis → malefic.

---

## Adhyaya 27 — Pravrajya (Ascetic) Yogas

Planetary conditions conducive to renunciation:
- Four or more planets in one sign (especially Jupiter-dominant) → strong ascetic urge
- Moon in drekkana of Saturn, aspected by Saturn → solitary contemplation
- Specific yogas listed that make a person "a successful ascetic revered by virtuous."

---

## Adhyaya 28 — Author's Colophon (Shloka-kramanika)

Mantreswara closes with:
- A verse-by-verse index of all 28 adhyayas
- His family (Sukuntala-mba lineage)
- Dedication to Lord Shiva, the Sun, and the Muses
- Stating the work is rooted in Parashara's tradition, condensed from Brihat-Jataka and Saravali with original synthesis on yogas and dasha interpretation.

## Why Phaladeepika Matters to a Modern Astrology App

1. **Yoga engine** — Adh. 6, 7, 18 are the canonical source for 200+ classical yogas used by every modern Vedic astrology software (Ruchaka, Bhadra, Gaja-Kesari, Neechabhanga, Raja-yogas, Dhana-yogas).
2. **Dasha interpretation rules** — Adh. 19–21 provide the authoritative logic for Mahadasha/Antardasha effect synthesis (karaka + ownership + aspect + placement).
3. **Ashtakavarga thresholds** — Adh. 23–24 define the binary rules (>28 = strong sign, <28 = weak) used by our Ashtakvarga tab.
4. **Transit (Gochara) rules** — Adh. 26 contains the good/bad transit house matrix for each planet — directly feeds a daily horoscope engine.
5. **Shadbala weights** — Adh. 4 is the basis of every Shadbala calculator, including the one in our Kundli module.
6. **Professional / wealth indicators** — Adh. 5's 10th-house-Navamsa rules are the classical logic behind Astro Rattan's career reports.
